Word Roots and Suffixes

Many English words are made from smaller parts. Understanding word roots and suffixes helps you guess meanings, recognize word families, and learn vocabulary more efficiently.

Word Roots

A word root is the main part of a word. It carries the core meaning. Many roots come from Latin or Greek and appear in many related words.

Root Meaning Examples
act do action, active, react
bio life biology, biography
port carry transport, export, portable
If you know the root port, you can understand many words related to carrying or moving things.

Suffixes

A suffix is added to the end of a word. Suffixes often change the word‚Äôs grammar form or add meaning.

Suffix Use / Meaning Examples
-er person who does something teacher, driver
-ness state or quality happiness, kindness
-ful full of helpful, careful
-less without careless, homeless

Root + Suffix

Roots and suffixes work together to form new words. The root meaning stays similar, but the suffix changes how the word is used.

care + -ful ‚Üí careful
care + -less ‚Üí careless
act + -ive ‚Üí active

Why This Is Important

  • Helps you understand long or unfamiliar words
  • Improves reading comprehension
  • Makes vocabulary learning faster
  • Helps you recognize word families

Learning Tip

When you see a new word, look for the root first, then the suffix. Ask yourself what each part means.
