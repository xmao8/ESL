Returning a Product

Characters:

  • ANNA: A determined shopper in her 30s.
  • CLERK: A friendly, but busy store employee.
  • MR. LEE: A kind, older man who is also a customer.

Setting: A bustling mall department store. A long line has formed at the customer service counter.

(The scene opens with ANNA standing in a long line, clutching a small, brightly-colored box. She looks impatient. She finally reaches the front.)

ANNA: Hello. I need to return this. The color isn't right.

CLERK: (Without looking up, scanning the box) Do you have the **receipt**?

ANNA: (Frantically searching her bag) Uh… yes, I think so. Wait… here it is! (She holds up a crumpled piece of paper.)

CLERK: (Taking the receipt and the box) Okay. Let’s see… Oh, no. This receipt is for another item. It's for a pair of socks.

ANNA: (Confused) Socks? No, no. This is the right one! I bought the… the… "blouse of the sun." No, that's not it. The "sunny shirt." Ah, "**sunrise shirt!**" That's it!

CLERK: (Shaking her head patiently) Ma'am, the receipt says "Happy Feet Socks." Do you have the **original receipt** for this sunrise shirt?

(ANNA's face falls. She continues to search her bag, but her frustration is growing. Behind her, MR. LEE, a quiet man in his 60s, waits patiently.)

ANNA: I am so sorry. I… I can’t find it. I am very tired. My brain is not working. The receipt must be at home.

CLERK: (Sympathetically) Without a receipt, I can only offer **store credit**. Is that okay?

ANNA: No! I don't want store credit. I need the money back. My husband will be very angry. He said, "Anna, don't lose the receipt!"

(ANNA starts to look tearful. MR. LEE steps forward, a kind smile on his face.)

MR. LEE: (To Anna) Excuse me. I think I have an idea. Maybe you paid with a **credit card**?

ANNA: (Looking up, surprised) Yes! I did. My husband’s card. Why?

MR. LEE: (To the Clerk) Can she return the item using the credit card information? I think some stores can look up the purchase that way.

CLERK: (Looking at Mr. Lee, then Anna) That's a great suggestion. We can try. It's not always possible, but let me see. Do you have the credit card with you?

ANNA: (Nodding eagerly) Yes! Yes! I do! (She quickly pulls out a credit card.)

(The CLERK takes the card and scans it. She types on the computer, and a moment later, she smiles.)

CLERK: Perfect! I found it. You're in luck! I can process the **refund** directly to your husband's card.

ANNA: (Relieved, she sighs loudly.) Thank you! Oh, thank you so much! (She looks at Mr. Lee.) And thank you! You are very smart! You saved my life. And my marriage!

MR. LEE: (Chuckles) You're very welcome. It was a good team **effort**.

(ANNA smiles, her face bright and happy again. She shakes Mr. Lee’s hand warmly as the Clerk processes the refund.)

Vocabulary List

  • Receipt 🔊 /rɪˈsiːt/ (n.): A printed slip of paper that shows you have paid for something. Example: The store clerk gave me a **receipt** after I paid.
  • Original receipt 🔊 /əˈrɪdʒənəl rɪˈsiːt/ (n.): The first receipt given when an item was purchased. Example: You need the **original receipt** to return the product.
  • Store credit 🔊 /stɔːr ˈkrɛdɪt/ (n.): Money that a store gives back to a customer, which can only be used at that specific store. Example: Without the receipt, they could only give me **store credit** instead of a refund.
  • Credit card 🔊 /ˈkrɛdɪt kɑːrd/ (n.): A plastic card that allows you to buy things and pay for them later. Example: I paid for the new shoes with my **credit card**.
  • Refund 🔊 /ˈriːfʌnd/ (n.): An amount of money that is paid back to you. Example: I got a full **refund** for the broken television.
  • In luck 🔊 /ɪn lʌk/ (idiom): To have good fortune. Example: I thought I was late, but I was **in luck** because the bus was delayed.
  • Process 🔊 /ˈprɑːsɛs/ (v.): To handle or deal with something using a specific procedure. Example: It takes a few days to **process** a passport application.
  • Effort 🔊 /ˈɛfərt/ (n.): The use of physical or mental energy to do something. Example: It took a lot of **effort** to lift the heavy box.
  • Frantically 🔊 /ˈfræntɪkli/ (adv.): In a hurried, disorganized, or desperate way. Example: She searched **frantically** for her keys.
