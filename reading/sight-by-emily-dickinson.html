SIGHT

by Emily Dickinson


Before I got my eye put out,

I liked as well to see

As other creatures that have eyes,

And know no other way.

But were it told to me, to-day,

That I might have the sky

For mine, I tell you that my heart

Would split, for size of me.

The meadows mine, the mountains mine, ‚Äî

All forests, stintless stars,

As much of noon as I could take

Between my finite eyes.

The motions of the dipping birds,

The lightning's jointed road,

For mine to look at when I liked, ‚Äî

The news would strike me dead!

So safer, guess, with just my soul

Upon the window-pane

Where other creatures put their eyes,

Incautious of the sun.

Vocabulary List

Here are important words from the poem. Try to guess the meaning first, then check the definitions.

  • Creatures
    Animals or people. In this context, living beings.
    "All creatures great and small live on this planet."
  • Stintless
    Without limit; boundless. This is a rare word that means without stinting or holding back.
    "The artist used a stintless amount of paint, creating a large, colorful masterpiece."
  • Finite
    Having an end or a limit.
    "Human life is finite, but art can be eternal."
  • Dipping
    Plunging or dropping down quickly.
    "We watched the birds dipping into the water to catch fish."
  • Jointed
    Having joints or connections, like a series of parts connected together.
    "The lightning bolt looked like a jointed line across the sky."
  • Incautious
    Not careful or cautious; reckless.
    "He was incautious and forgot to look both ways before crossing the street."

Comprehension Questions

Answer these questions about the poem "SIGHT."

  1. What does the speaker mean by "Before I got my eye put out"?
  2. In the second stanza, why would the speaker's heart "split, for size of me" if they could see the sky?
  3. What natural things does the speaker mention in the third and fourth stanzas that they now cannot see?
  4. What does the speaker compare the path of lightning to?
  5. How does the speaker feel about not being able to see all of these things anymore?
  6. What is the main idea or message of the final stanza?
