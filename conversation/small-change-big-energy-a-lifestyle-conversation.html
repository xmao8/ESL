Small Changes, Big Energy

Characters:

  • LINA: Dental assistant who wants more energy.
  • MARCOS: Rideshare driver improving his diet on a budget.
  • AYUMI: Graduate student balancing research and yoga.

Scene 1: Morning at a community café. Soft music, laptops open.

Scene 1

LINA: I slept six hours again. My mornings feel heavy.

MARCOS: Same here. Night shifts plus late snacks… not ideal.

AYUMI: I used to feel that way. Now I protect my evening routine.

LINA: What do you do?

AYUMI: Lights down at ten, phone outside the bedroom, and a short stretch.

MARCOS: I drive until midnight. If I stop eating chips, I get hungry.

AYUMI: Try portioned snacks—nuts or fruit. Planned beats surprise cravings.

LINA: My problem is mornings. I hit snooze and rush.

MARCOS: Let’s test one change this week.

Scene 2

Later, walking in a park.

LINA: I’ll walk fifteen minutes after lunch. Sunshine wakes me up.

MARCOS: I’ll pack a snack box and water.

AYUMI: I’ll add a rest day. Rest is fuel, not laziness.

MARCOS: Two stretch breaks between rides, no chips after 9 p.m.

LINA: Lights off at 10:30 and “Do Not Disturb.”

AYUMI: Goals must be specific and realistic.

Scene 3

A week later, same café.

MARCOS: Snack box saved money and my mood. I still craved sugar, but nuts helped.

LINA: Lunch walks changed my afternoons. Four days out of seven!

AYUMI: My screen-free night felt quiet, then peaceful. I slept deeper.

MARCOS: I grabbed fries once, but moved on.

LINA: Progress, not perfection.

AYUMI: Next steps?

LINA: Meal prep on Sundays—two simple dinners.

MARCOS: Keep water in the car and add a five-minute breathing break.

AYUMI: Protect bedtime, even during exams.

LINA: Small changes add up.

MARCOS: Big energy, small steps.

ALL: Move a little, plan a little, rest enough!

— THE END —

Vocabulary List

  • Routine /ruːˈtiːn/ (n.) a regular way of doing things. Example: An evening routine can improve sleep.
  • Portion /ˈpɔːrʃn/ (n./v.) an amount of food; to divide food into amounts. Example: She portioned nuts before work.
  • Sedentary /ˈsedntri/ (adj.) involving little physical activity. Example: A sedentary job can cause back pain.
  • Hydrate /ˈhaɪdreɪt/ (v.) drink enough water. Example: Drivers hydrate between rides.
  • Burnout /ˈbɜːrnaʊt/ (n.) extreme tiredness from stress. Example: Rest days help prevent burnout.
  • Unwind /ʌnˈwaɪnd/ (v.) relax after work. Example: She unwinds with light stretching.
  • Meal prep /miːl prep/ (n.) cooking food in advance. Example: Meal prep saves money and time.
  • Moderation /ˌmɒdəˈreɪʃn/ (n.) not too much or too little of something. Example: Snacks are fine in moderation.
  • Stamina /ˈstæmɪnə/ (n.) energy to keep going for a long time. Example: Walks increased her stamina.
  • Mindfulness /ˈmaɪndf(ʊ)lnəs/ (n.) calm, focused attention on the present. Example: Five mindful breaths reduced stress.
  • Craving /ˈkreɪvɪŋ/ (n.) a strong desire for something, usually food. Example: Fruit helped with sugar cravings.
  • Flexible schedule /ˈfleksɪb(ə)l ˈʃedjuːl/ (n.) a plan of hours that can change. Example: A flexible schedule made yoga possible.
  • Bedtime /ˈbedtaɪm/ (n.) the time you go to bed. Example: A steady bedtime improved sleep.
  • Balance /ˈbæl.əns/ (n./v.) a healthy mix of activities. Example: She balanced study, work, and rest.
  • Habit /ˈhæbɪt/ (n.) something you do regularly, often without thinking. Example: A short daily walk became a habit.
