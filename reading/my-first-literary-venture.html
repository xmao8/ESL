Vocabulary List

Here are important words and phrases from the story. Try to guess the meaning first, then check the definitions.

  • Scribbling
    Writing or drawing in a careless or messy way. In this context, it refers to his early, unprofessional writing.
    "My first attempt at writing was just scribbling in a notebook."
  • Printer's ‚Äúdevil‚Äù
    An apprentice in a printing office who does the dirty work.
    "Before he became a great writer, he started as a printer's 'devil'."
  • Judiciously
    With good judgment and wisdom.
    "She spent her money judiciously, saving a little each month."
  • Jilted
    Suddenly rejected or abandoned a lover.
    "He felt sad and angry after he was jilted by his girlfriend."
  • Moral obliquity
    A deviation from what is considered morally right; moral wrongdoing.
    "The newspaper reporter was criticized for his moral obliquity in writing a false story."
  • Parody
    An imitation of the style of a particular writer, artist, or genre with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect.
    "The movie was a parody of classic action films."
  • Lampooned
    Publicly criticized or ridiculed with satire.
    "The political cartoon lampooned the politician for his foolish speech."
  • Simpering coxcomb
    A foolish, vain man who smiles or speaks in a silly, affected way.
    "He was such a simpering coxcomb, always bragging about his clothes."
  • Incensed
    Very angry; enraged.
    "She was incensed by the rude comments he made."
  • Impetus
    The force or energy with which a body moves; a driving force.
    "His speech provided the impetus for the new project."
  • Animosity
    Strong hostility.
    "There was a lot of animosity between the two rival teams."

Comprehension Questions

Answer these questions about the story "My First Literary Venture."

  1. What was the narrator's job at the beginning of the story?
  2. Who was Higgins, and what was the funny story about him that the narrator wrote about?
  3. What other things did the narrator write about to make the paper "lively"?
  4. What was the funny footnote about the tailor's poetry?
  5. How did the people the narrator wrote about react to the newspaper?
  6. Why was the narrator's uncle initially angry, and what made him change his mind?
  7. What is the overall tone or feeling of this story?
