Interview at Runway Magazine

Characters:

  • MIRANDA: Editor-in-Chief at Runway Magazine, powerful and direct.
  • ANDY: An aspiring journalist, intelligent but initially out of place.

MIRANDA: Who are you?

ANDY: My name is Andy Sachs. I recently graduated from Northwestern University.

MIRANDA: And what are you doing here?

ANDY: Well, I think I could do a good job as your assistant. And… I came to New York to be a journalist. I sent letters out everywhere, and then finally got a call from Elias Clarke and met with Sherry up at Human Resources. And... basically, it’s this or Auto Universe.

MIRANDA: So, you don’t read Runway?

ANDY: Oh, no!

MIRANDA: And before today, you had never heard of me?

ANDY: No.

MIRANDA: And you have no style or sense of fashion.

ANDY: Well, I think that depends on what you’re…

MIRANDA: (cutting her off) No, no. That wasn’t a question.

ANDY: Um… I was editor-in-chief of The Daily Northwestern. I also won a national competition for college journalists with my series on the janitors' union, which exposed the exploitation of the—

MIRANDA: (interrupting) That’s all.

(Pause. Andy stands to leave.)

ANDY: Yeah, yeah, okay. You’re right. I don’t fit in here. I’m not skinny or glamorous, and I don’t know that much about fashion. But I’m smart. I learn fast, and I will work very hard…

(Silence)

ANDY: Thank you for your time.

MIRANDA: (to Emily) Who is that sad little person?

Discussion Questions

  • What kind of job is Andy applying for?
  • What is Miranda's initial impression of Andy?
  • What are Andy's strengths, according to her?
  • Why does Andy say "it's this or Auto Universe"? What does that tell you about her situation?
  • How does Miranda's way of speaking differ from Andy's?
  • Have you ever been in an interview where you felt you didn't "fit in"?

Vocabulary List

  • graduated 🔊: completed a course of study at a school, college, or university.
    • Example: She graduated with a degree in literature.
    • Example: Andy recently graduated from Northwestern University.
  • journalist 🔊: a person who writes for newspapers, magazines, or news websites, or prepares news to be broadcast.
    • Example: My dream is to become a foreign correspondent journalist.
    • Example: Andy came to New York to be a journalist.
  • Human Resources (HR) 🔊: the department of a company or organization that deals with hiring, training, and managing employees.
    • Example: You should contact Human Resources about your benefits.
    • Example: Andy met with Sherry up at Human Resources.
  • cutting her off 🔊: interrupting someone while they are speaking.
    • Example: It's rude to keep cutting people off when they're talking.
    • Example: Miranda kept cutting Andy off during the interview.
  • editor-in-chief 🔊: the person in charge of a newspaper, magazine, or publication.
    • Example: The editor-in-chief made the final decision on the article.
    • Example: Andy was editor-in-chief of The Daily Northwestern.
  • exposed 🔊: revealed something hidden or secret.
    • Example: The documentary exposed the truth about the company.
    • Example: Her series exposed the exploitation of the janitors' union.
  • exploitation 🔊: the action or fact of treating someone unfairly in order to benefit from their work.
    • Example: The film highlighted the exploitation of migrant workers.
    • Example: Andy's series exposed the exploitation of the union.
  • fit in 🔊: to be accepted by a group of people and feel comfortable among them.
    • Example: It can be hard to fit in when you move to a new school.
    • Example: Andy felt she didn't fit in at Runway Magazine.
  • glamorous 🔊: having an exciting and attractive quality.
    • Example: She lives a very glamorous life in Hollywood.
    • Example: Andy felt she wasn't glamorous enough for the job.
