Talk about Weather

Characters:
  • Maria: From a country with four seasons and cold winters
  • Juan: From a country with a warm, tropical, or desert climate
(Setting: A break room or cafeteria. Maria is looking out the window.)
Maria: Oh, look! It's so cloudy today.
Juan: (Walks over to the window) Yes, it is. Do you think it will rain?
Maria: Hmm, maybe. The air feels a little heavy. I hope not. I walked here today.
Juan: Oh, me too! I forgot my umbrella. What's the weather usually like here in June?
Maria: Well, June is usually warm and sunny. We get a lot of sunshine. But sometimes we have a few rainy days, especially in the early part of the month.
Juan: That's interesting. Where I'm from, it's always hot. We have a rainy season and a dry season, but it's never really cold.
Maria: Wow, never cold? That's hard for me to imagine! In my country, we have four seasons. Winter is very cold, sometimes with snow.
Juan: Snow! I've only seen snow in movies. Is it nice?
Maria: It can be very beautiful, especially when it first falls. But it also makes driving difficult, and it can be very, very cold. I prefer spring.
Juan: Why spring?
Maria: Because the weather is mild. It's not too hot, not too cold. The flowers start to bloom, and the days get longer. It's lovely.
Juan: Sounds nice. I like warm weather. For me, the perfect day is sunny and about 25 degrees Celsius.
Maria: Yes, that's a good temperature. Today, it's only about 18 degrees. A bit cool for June, I think.
Juan: Yes, a little cool. But it's good for studying, not too hot to focus.
Maria: True! Maybe the sun will come out later.
Juan: I hope so!
