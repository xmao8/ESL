English Articles: Fill in the Blank

Instructions: Read each sentence carefully. Fill in the blank with the correct article (a, an, the) or leave it blank (X) if no article is needed. These sentences are about daily life activities.

Exercise 1: Complete the Sentences

  1. Every morning, I drink cup of coffee.
  2. My sister is engineer. She works for big tech company.
  3. We usually have breakfast at 7:00 AM.
  4. I saw interesting movie last night. movie was about history of jazz.
  5. Could you please pass me salt? It's right next to bread.
  6. sun rises in east.
  7. My neighbor bought new car. It's red sports car.
  8. I love listening to music while I clean house.
  9. She is best student in class.
  10. Do you want to go to park this afternoon?
  11. He is learning to play guitar.
  12. I need advice about my new job.
  13. birds sing beautifully in morning.
  14. We went to beach last weekend. water was cold.
  15. Can you recommend good restaurant in this area?

Exercise 2: Short Paragraph

Read the paragraph below and fill in the blanks with "a," "an," "the," or "X" (no article).

Yesterday, I had busy day. First, I woke up early and ate quick breakfast. Then, I drove to office, which is located in city center. I had important meeting with client. After meeting, I had lunch with colleagues. In afternoon, I went to gym and did hour of exercise. When I got home, I watched TV for little while and then read book before going to bed. It was long day, but productive one.

  3. We usually have **X** breakfast at 7:00 AM.
  4. I saw **an** interesting movie last night. **The** movie was about **the** history of jazz.

Vocabulary List

Here are 15 important words from the article that can help you understand it better. Try to guess their meaning from the story first, then check the definitions.

  • Disoriented
    Confused about where you are or what direction to go.
    Example: After spinning around for too long, she felt completely **disoriented**.
  • Startled
    Suddenly surprised or frightened.
    Example: The loud thunder **startled** the baby, and she cried.
  • Squeezed
    To fit or force something into a small space.
    Example: He **squeezed** through the crowded train doors.
  • Heartbroken
    Very sad and disappointed.
    Example: She was **heartbroken** when her favorite plant died.
  • Meanwhile
    At the same time.
    Example: He was studying for his exam; ** meanwhile**, his friends were playing outside.
  • Incredible
    Very difficult to believe; amazing.
    Example: The sunset over the ocean was an **incredible** sight.
  • Hitch rides
    To get free rides in other people's vehicles.
    Example: In college, he used to **hitch rides** to save money.
  • Perils
    Serious and immediate dangers.
    Example: Explorers often face many **perils** when venturing into unknown territories.
  • Survivor
    A person or animal who continues to live after an accident, illness, or difficult experience.
    Example: After the car crash, she was grateful to be a **survivor**.
  • Mystery
    Something that is difficult or impossible to understand or explain.
    Example: How the ancient pyramids were built is still a **mystery**.
  • Claim
    To say that something belongs to you.
    Example: No one came to **claim** the lost umbrella.
  • Microchip
    A tiny electronic device implanted under an animal's skin containing identification information.
    Example: The vet scanned the dog for a **microchip** to find its owner.
  • Beloved
    Much loved; dear.
    Example: She spent the day with her **beloved** grandparents.
  • Overjoyed
    Extremely happy.
    Example: They were **overjoyed** to hear the good news.
  • Resilience
    The ability to recover quickly from difficulties; toughness.
    Example: Children often show amazing **resilience** after challenging times.

Comprehension Questions

Answer these questions about the article "From Lost to Found: A Stray Dog's Amazing Journey."

  1. Where did Buddy live with his family before he got **disoriented**, and what kind of dog was he?
  2. How long was Buddy lost before he was found, and in which state was he found?
  3. What important device did the animal shelter use to find Buddy's family?
  4. Why was Buddy's family described as "heartbroken" and then "overjoyed"?
  5. What are two things Buddy's story reminds us about, according to the article?
  6. How do you think Buddy survived all the **perils** of his long journey of over 1,500 miles? (This answer is not directly in the text; use your imagination based on the hints!)
