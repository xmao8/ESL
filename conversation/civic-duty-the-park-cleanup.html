Civic Duty: The Park Cleanup

Characters:

  • ANNA: A new high school student, 16 years old. She is hesitant and a little shy.
  • LEO: Anna's classmate, 16 years old. He is enthusiastic and organized.
  • MR. JENKINS: A kind, older man, the park's caretaker.

Scene: A local community park. It's a sunny Saturday morning. There are a few bags of trash and some rakes nearby.

Scene 1

ANNA: Leo, are you sure about this? It's a beautiful day. We could be doing anything else right now.

LEO: (Smiling) Of course, I'm sure! It's our city's "Community Service Day." Plus, Mr. Jenkins needs our help. He takes care of this whole park by himself. It's a **civic responsibility**.

ANNA: What does that really mean? "Civic responsibility." Is it just... a fancy name for work?

LEO: (Putting the map away) No. It's about being an active **citizen** in our **community**. It means we have a **duty** to make our city better. It's not just about our **rights**, like voting or speaking freely. It's also about helping.

MR. JENKINS: Good morning, you two! Thank you for **volunteering**. I saw the **petition** you started last month to get more funding for the park. That was wonderful.

ANNA: (Blushing) Oh, that was Leo's idea. I just signed it.

LEO: She helped me **advocate** for it online! It's a team effort. You know, Mr. Jenkins, we learned about government **policy** in class. We want to **contribute** to making things better, not just complain.

MR. JENKINS: That's the spirit! It's this kind of **participation** that keeps a community strong. This park is for everyone. It's a place for families to spend time together. It’s a great example of being **cooperative**.

ANNA: (Looking around) So, what's our job? Picking up trash?

MR. JENKINS: Yes, and raking leaves. We need to **protect** this place. It’s our shared space.

ANNA: (Puts her phone in her pocket. She picks up a rake and starts raking a pile of leaves.) Okay, I get it now. It's not just work. It's like... we're taking care of our home.

LEO: Exactly! And we're setting an example for others. Maybe next year more people will **serve** with us.

MR. JENKINS: You are both doing a great **service**. This is what makes a democracy successful. When we all **obey** the laws and **respect** our shared spaces, we can make this a great place to live. Thank you.

ANNA: Let’s start over there, Leo. We can finish this fast.

**THE END**

Vocabulary List

  • Citizen 🔊 /ˈsɪtɪzən/ (n.) A person who belongs to a country and has certain rights and duties. Example: He is a citizen of this country.
  • Community 🔊 /kəˈmjuːnəti/ (n.) A group of people living in the same place or having a particular characteristic in common. Example: We should help our local community.
  • Responsibility 🔊 /rɪˌspɑːnsəˈbɪləti/ (n.) A duty or obligation to do something. Example: Voting is a civic responsibility.
  • Rights 🔊 /raɪts/ (n.) Moral or legal entitlements to have or obtain something or to act in a certain way. Example: Everyone has the right to an education.
  • Duty 🔊 /ˈduːti/ (n.) A moral or legal obligation. Example: It's our duty to respect the law.
  • Volunteer 🔊 /ˌvɑːlənˈtɪər/ (v.) To offer to do something without being paid. Example: We volunteer at the hospital on weekends.
  • Petition 🔊 /pəˈtɪʃən/ (n.) A formal written request, signed by many people, appealing to authority. Example: They started a petition to change the park rules.
  • Advocate 🔊 /ˈædvəkeɪt/ (v.) To publicly support a particular cause or policy. Example: She advocates for animal rights.
  • Policy 🔊 /ˈpɑːləsi/ (n.) A course or principle of action adopted or proposed by a government. Example: The new government policy will affect many people.
  • Contribute 🔊 /kənˈtrɪbjuːt/ (v.) To give something, such as money, time, or effort, to help a cause. Example: Everyone can contribute to the team's success.
