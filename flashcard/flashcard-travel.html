Travel Vocabulary Flashcards

destination
destination (n.)
Place you plan to go.
Example: Our destination is Rome.
itinerary
itinerary (n.)
Planned route or schedule.
Example: I received my travel itinerary by email.
accommodation
accommodation (n.)
Place to stay during travel.
Example: We booked our accommodation near the beach.
passport
passport (n.)
Official travel document.
Example: Don‚Äôt forget to renew your passport.
visa
visa (n.)
Entry permit for a country.
Example: She applied for a tourist visa.
luggage
luggage (n.)
Suitcases and bags.
Example: Your luggage is limited to one bag.
reservation
reservation (n.)
Booking in advance.
Example: I made a reservation at the hotel.
sightseeing
sightseeing (n.)
Visiting famous places.
Example: We went sightseeing in Paris.
currency
currency (n.)
Money used in a country.
Example: Exchange currency before you travel.
transportation
transportation (n.)
Means of travel.
Example: We used public transportation downtown.
boarding pass
boarding pass (n.)
Ticket for boarding a plane.
Example: Keep your boarding pass handy.
excursion
excursion (n.)
Short trip for pleasure.
Example: We took an excursion to the islands.
guidebook
guidebook (n.)
Book of travel information.
Example: I bought a guidebook for Tokyo.
backpacker
backpacker (n.)
Traveler with a backpack.
Example: She traveled Europe like a backpacker.
landmark
landmark (n.)
Famous building or place.
Example: The Eiffel Tower is a landmark.
adventure
adventure (n.)
Exciting experience.
Example: Our hiking trip was a great adventure.
journey
journey (n.)
Travel from one place to another.
Example: The journey took all day.
backpack
backpack (v.& n.)
Carry gear in a bag; the bag itself.
Example: He backpacked across Asia.
trip
trip (n.)
Journey or excursion.
Example: We planned a weekend trip.
tour
tour (n.)
Guided visit of a place.
Example: We took a city tour.
cruise
cruise (n.)
Trip on a ship.
Example: They enjoyed a Caribbean cruise.
check-in
check-in (n.)
Process of registering for travel.
Example: Check-in opens two hours before flight.
departure
departure (n.)
When you leave a place.
Example: The departure was delayed.
arrival
arrival (n.)
When you reach a place.
Example: Our arrival was on time.
souvenir
souvenir (n.)
Item to remember a place.
Example: She bought a souvenir magnet.
