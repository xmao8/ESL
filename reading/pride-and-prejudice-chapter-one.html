Pride and Prejudice — Chapter One

Pride and Prejudice is a famous novel by Jane Austen. In Chapter One, readers meet the Bennet family. The chapter begins with one of the most well-known opening lines in English literature. It introduces the idea that a rich single man is expected to need a wife. This sentence is a little humorous, and it shows that the novel will talk about marriage, society, and family expectations.

Mrs. Bennet is very excited because a wealthy young man named Mr. Bingley has rented a large house called Netherfield Park. She immediately thinks about her five unmarried daughters. She hopes one of them can marry him. Mrs. Bennet is energetic, emotional, and strongly focused on finding husbands for her daughters.

Mr. Bennet, her husband, reacts very differently. He is calm, quiet, and often speaks with dry humor. In this chapter, he enjoys teasing his wife. Mrs. Bennet tells him that he must visit Mr. Bingley as soon as possible. In their society, a formal visit is important because it creates a proper introduction between families.

Mrs. Bennet becomes frustrated because Mr. Bennet does not seem interested. She complains that he does not care about his daughters’ future. However, Mr. Bennet continues joking with her instead of giving a clear answer. Their conversation is playful, but it also reveals their very different personalities. Mrs. Bennet is nervous and dramatic, while Mr. Bennet is clever and amused.

The chapter does not give much information yet about the daughters themselves, but it helps build the setting of the story. Readers see a world where money, marriage, and social connections are very important. A rich new neighbor can create excitement for an entire family.

At the end of the chapter, the conversation between Mr. and Mrs. Bennet leaves readers curious. Will Mr. Bennet visit Mr. Bingley? Will one of the daughters have a chance to meet him? Jane Austen uses this first chapter to introduce the family, establish the tone, and begin the novel with wit and interest.

Vocabulary List

  • wealthy — Having a lot of money; rich.
  • rent — To pay money to use a house, room, or land for a period of time.
  • unmarried — Not married.
  • expectation — A belief about what will or should happen.
  • formal — Following official or traditional rules.
  • introduction — The act of meeting someone for the first time.
  • frustrated — Feeling annoyed because something is not happening as hoped.
  • tease — To joke about someone in a playful way.
  • dramatic — Showing strong feelings in an exaggerated way.
  • wit — The ability to say clever and funny things.
  • society — People living together in an organized community with shared customs.
  • connection — A relationship or link between people.
  • curious — Wanting to know more about something.
  • tone — The general feeling or style of a piece of writing.
  • neighbor — A person who lives nearby.
