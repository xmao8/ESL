Pride and Prejudice — Chapter Ten (Abridged)

The day at Netherfield passed quietly. Jane continued to recover, and Elizabeth spent much of her time with her. In the evening, she joined the others in the drawing room. Mr. Darcy was writing a letter, while Miss Bingley sat beside him, trying to attract his attention. She repeatedly interrupted him with compliments and messages to his sister, praising his handwriting and asking him to add more to his letter. Darcy responded politely but remained focused on his writing.

Miss Bingley admired everything Darcy did and tried to impress him, but her efforts were not very successful. Her brother, Mr. Bingley, joked about his own careless writing style, saying that his ideas moved too quickly for him to express clearly. Elizabeth responded humorously, suggesting that his modesty protected him from criticism. Darcy disagreed and argued that apparent modesty is often only a form of pride or indirect boasting. He explained that people sometimes take pride in their weaknesses if they believe those weaknesses make them interesting.

The conversation continued with Darcy criticizing Bingley’s tendency to act too quickly. He suggested that acting without careful thought could lead to mistakes and unfinished responsibilities. Bingley laughed and admitted that he might dislike long arguments, preferring a lighter and more relaxed discussion. Elizabeth agreed to end the debate and encouraged Darcy to finish his letter.

After completing his writing, Darcy suggested having some music. Miss Bingley quickly went to the piano and began to play, hoping to impress him. Elizabeth listened quietly and noticed that Darcy often looked at her. She was unsure why he paid attention to her, since she believed he did not admire her. She assumed he was simply finding fault with her, but this did not trouble her.

Later, Darcy approached Elizabeth and asked if she wished to dance. She understood that he expected her to agree so that he could judge her taste. Instead, she refused playfully, saying she preferred to surprise him and avoid giving him the chance to criticize her. Darcy responded with politeness and did not appear offended. Elizabeth was surprised by his reaction, as she had expected to annoy him.

In truth, Darcy was becoming more interested in Elizabeth. He admired her lively personality, intelligence, and playful manner. Although he still noticed the lower social position of her family, he found himself increasingly attracted to her. Miss Bingley, observing this growing interest, became jealous. She hoped for Jane’s recovery, not only out of concern, but also because she wished Elizabeth would leave Netherfield.

Vocabulary List

  • interrupt — to stop someone while they are speaking or working.
  • compliment — a polite expression of praise.
  • modesty — not thinking too highly of oneself.
  • boasting — speaking with pride about oneself.
  • criticize — to point out faults.
  • responsibility — something you must do.
  • politeness — showing good manners.
  • assume — to think something is true without proof.
  • refuse — to say no.
  • attracted — feeling interest or liking.
