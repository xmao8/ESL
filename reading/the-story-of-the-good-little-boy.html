Vocabulary List

Here are important words and phrases from the story. Try to guess the meaning first, then check the definitions.

  • Absurd
    Unreasonable, illogical, or ridiculous.
    "It was an absurd request to ask him to finish all that work in one hour."
  • Sabbath-school
    A religious school for children, usually held on Sunday.
    "The children all went to Sabbath-school to learn about the Bible."
  • Play hookey
    To stay away from school without permission; to be truant.
    "The mischievous boy decided to play hookey and go to the park."
  • Magnanimously
    Generously and with a noble spirit, especially towards a rival or someone less powerful.
    "She magnanimously offered to help her opponent after the competition."
  • Welted
    Hit hard, often leaving a mark or a welt.
    "The bully welted the boy on the arm with a stick."
  • Consolation
    Something that makes a person feel better after a disappointment or loss.
    "His kind words were a great consolation to me during my sadness."
  • Imperishable
    Lasting forever; not subject to decay.
    "The hero's name was carved in stone, a symbol of his imperishable fame."
  • Consternation
    A feeling of alarm, anxiety, or dismay, often caused by something unexpected.
    "To my great consternation, I realized I had left my wallet at home."
  • Dumfounded
    So surprised that one is unable to speak; astonished.
    "He was dumfounded when he saw the surprise party his friends had planned."
  • Admonish
    To warn or scold someone firmly but gently.
    "The teacher had to admonish the students for being too loud in the library."
  • Inquest
    An official investigation to determine the facts of an incident, especially a death.
    "The police held an inquest to figure out the cause of the fire."
