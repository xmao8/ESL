Healthy Habits, Healthy Life

Characters:

  • ANNA: A young professional who works long hours. She's often tired.
  • BEN: Anna's colleague and friend. He is energetic and into fitness.
  • MARIA: A retired neighbor. She is kind and wise.

The scene opens in a park on a sunny Saturday morning. Anna is sitting on a bench, scrolling on her phone. Ben jogs up to her, full of energy.

BEN: Anna! You made it! I didn't think you'd actually get out of bed.

ANNA: Hey, Ben. My alarm went off, and I almost hit snooze. I just feel so tired all the time. Work has been crazy.

BEN: That’s exactly why we’re here! A little fresh air will do you good. Come on, let’s do a quick stretch.

(Anna reluctantly stands up and imitates Ben's stretches. Maria, a kind-looking older woman, walks by with a small bag of groceries.)

MARIA: Good morning, you two! It’s a beautiful day, isn’t it?

BEN: Good morning, Maria! It certainly is.

ANNA: Hi, Maria.

MARIA: You seem a little low on energy, dear. Is everything alright?

ANNA: I’m just so exhausted. I work late, I eat dinner on my sofa, and then I fall asleep with my laptop still on. I know it’s not good, but I don’t have time for anything else.

MARIA: I remember those days. But our bodies are like gardens. If we don’t take care of them, the flowers won’t grow. It’s not about having more time; it’s about using the time you have wisely.

BEN: Exactly! Like a little walk. Or drinking more water instead of coffee.

MARIA: And what you eat is so important. I walk to the market every morning to get fresh vegetables. A little soup, a little salad. It makes a big difference. It's my secret to having so much energy.

(Maria holds up her small grocery bag with a smile. Anna looks from Maria to Ben, and then around the park at the other people walking and running.)

ANNA: So, a walk, a few fresh vegetables… it sounds so simple.

BEN: It is! We don’t have to run a marathon every day. Just a small, good habit. A few minutes of stretching, a walk around the block after work.

MARIA: Think of it as an investment. You are investing in your own well-being. And the payoff is feeling good, having more energy.

ANNA: Okay. I can try that. Maybe I can walk home from work instead of taking the bus one day this week.

BEN: Great idea! And I can help you find some quick, healthy dinner recipes. No more eating on the sofa.

MARIA: That's the spirit! One small step leads to another. And before you know it, you’ll be a beautiful garden yourself.

(Maria smiles and continues on her way. Anna and Ben look at each other, then Anna takes a deep breath of fresh air.)

ANNA: Alright, Ben. Let’s do it. What’s our first "small step"?

(Ben grins, and they begin to do some light exercises together as the scene fades.)

(End of Scene)

Vocabulary List

  • Snooze 🔊 /snuːz/ (v.): To sleep for a short time, especially to press a button on an alarm clock to stop it from ringing for a few minutes. Example: He hit snooze three times this morning.
  • Fresh air 🔊 /frɛʃ ɛər/ (n.): The clean, cool air outside. Example: A walk in the park gives you fresh air and helps you feel better.
  • Stretching 🔊 /ˈstrɛtʃɪŋ/ (n.): The act of making your muscles longer by moving them into a position that makes them tight. Example: I do some stretching before I exercise to warm up.
  • Low on energy 🔊 /loʊ ɒn ˈɛnərdʒi/ (idiom): To feel tired and not have much physical or mental power. Example: After a long day at work, I was low on energy.
  • Exhausted 🔊 /ɪɡˈzɔːstɪd/ (adj.): Very tired. Example: He was exhausted after running the race.
  • Wisely 🔊 /ˈwaɪz.li/ (adv.): In a way that shows good judgment or knowledge. Example: She chose her healthy foods wisely.
  • Investment 🔊 /ɪnˈvɛst.mənt/ (n.): The act of putting money, effort, or time into something to get a good result. Example: Taking care of your health is a great investment.
  • Payoff 🔊 /ˈpeɪ.ɒf/ (n.): The good result or advantage that you get from a difficult situation. Example: The payoff for her hard work was a great grade.
